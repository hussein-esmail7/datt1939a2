3. Articulate your concepts and intentions in a brief explanation of, guiding questions for, and/or artist's statement about your completed work. Minimum Articulation word count: 300 words / Suggested Maximum: 700 words

In this assignment, our group decided to make a JavaScript-based game where the player would try to steal something. The player does not know what it is they are stealing until they reach the end of the game, except the clues that are left behind in the form of sticky notes. The location they are trying to rob from is the mansion of a rich man (who in the game is currently in his indoor pool in pool.html). The object that is hidden inside the vault is a painting that seems a bit worthless.

The possible outcomes of this game for the player all end similarly. If the player is caught by one of the characters such as the owner, kid, or dog, they go back to the entryway. If the player collects all three clues and gets to the vault, the player finally sees that the painting is not too interesting, even if it was painted by Leonardo da Vinci. So the player decides to put back the painting and give up their life of crime.

The reason why we made the game this way is to teach people that even though stealing may be beneficial for the robber, it is not the right thing to do. Originally during the planning process, an idea was tossed around that the player would be discovered as they are exiting the mansion with the painting, meaning that entering the mansion at all would mean an arrest would be made.

At the beginning of the planning process of this project, all of the project members knew that we wanted to do some sort of adventure game, since that would give something for the player to work for, thus they might enjoy it more once they see their supposed reward.

During the creation of this project, the work was mainly divided into 2 groups; design of the artwork and coding of the HTML pages. I (Hussein) feel that this was good on our part because some of us are better artists than programmers, and others have had previous programming knowledge.
